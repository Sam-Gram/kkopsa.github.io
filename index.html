Kevin Kopsa

Software Developer

In January 2012, I started my career as a software developer when I took my first programming class at BYU-Idaho while initially pursuing an Electrical Engineering degree. I realized quickly that I liked high-level programming languages much more than low-level circuit diagrams.

Since then, I have grown to love programming and software development even more.

Throughout my education, I held a couple of different positions. Firstly, I worked in software quality assurance as a tester for Surge™, a custom software development and consulting company. I gained experience as in software development methodology as well as technical knowledge of testing web and mobile applications.

Afterwards I continued my schooling for a few more semesters and later became a mobile developer for the same company. I gained experience with both Android and iOS mobile platforms.

Now, I am coming to the close of my formal education, and I have drawn a couple conclusions from my experiences. I love the world of mobile applications and their potential throughout the world. I love the idea of helping developing countries utilize this technology that has literally been placed in the hands of billions around the globe.

It is my dream to further mobile technology as a means to help developing countries throughout the world.
